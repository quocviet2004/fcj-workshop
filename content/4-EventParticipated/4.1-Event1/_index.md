# Summary Report: “FCAJ Community Day 2026”

### Event Objectives
- Sharing methods to "hack" the brain and trick it into learning more effectively
- Introducing and sharing effective ways to use AI
- Career orientation and sharing experiences for 3rd and 4th-year students preparing to graduate

### Speakers

- Mr. Huỳnh Hoàng Long
- Mr. Nguyễn Tuấn Thịnh
- Mr. Nguyễn Khang
- Ms. Thảo (Software Developer at VIB)

#### Identifying the drawbacks of legacy application architecture

- Long product release cycles → Lost revenue/missed opportunities  
- Inefficient operations → Reduced productivity, higher costs  
- Non-compliance with security regulations → Security breaches, loss of reputation  

#### Highlighted Content
1. How to hack/trick the brain to apply better in learning
Speaker: Mr. Huỳnh Hoàng Long

- Story: Playing games or scrolling social media feels fun and lasts a long time, but studying feels demotivating and boring.
- Solution: Apply the reward & loss method (e.g., when you complete a study session, reward yourself; if you don’t, you lose something — leveraging humans’ fear of loss).
- Do tasks immediately without hesitation. Start with easy tasks first.
- Make plans and break down big tasks into smaller ones.

2. Perspective and Sharing from a Recruiter
Speaker: Mr. Nguyễn Khang

- Technology is changing rapidly, so it’s not necessary to know everything. Instead, focus on mastering the core fundamentals, developing strong problem-solving thinking, and the ability to learn.
- Attitude is extremely important.
- Integrity is something everyone values and looks for.

3. AI Agent
Speaker: Ms. Thảo (Software Developer at VIB)

- Current situation: Asking ChatGPT randomly leads to lengthy, rambling answers without real knowledge, causing many difficulties.
- Proposed methods for using AI more effectively.
- BMX Method (BMX Method).
- Multi-Agent Ecosystem.
When used properly, AI understands your intent better, saves tokens, and makes accurate improvements exactly where needed.

#### Key Takeaways

- Self-Management: Discipline is not as effective as smart methods. Break tasks into small parts, do easy things first, and establish a clear reward/punishment system to “hack” the brain’s motivation.

- Career Orientation: Integrity, work attitude, and core problem-solving thinking are always more valuable than chasing after the surface of countless new technologies.

- Tool Optimization: You must communicate with AI in a structured way (such as the BMX method) instead of asking vaguely, so the AI can analyze the right context, solve problems accurately, and optimize performance.

#### Application in Work & Daily Life

- Interacting with AI (Prompt Engineering): When asking for help debugging CloudFormation configurations or fixing errors.
- CV & Interview Strategy: When preparing for interviews.
- Forcing work progress: Break work into small parts, do the easy ones first, then tackle the harder ones.
